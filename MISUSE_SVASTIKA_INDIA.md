## Linking Maus, Svastika, the Roman
Maus is a graphic novel by Art Spiegelman depicting the Holocaust through animal metaphors (Nazis as cats, Jews as mice). The Svastika, a sacred Hindu symbol meaning "auspicious", was appropriated by Nazis as their emblem, misusing its ancient Indian origins. The Roman refers to the Roman salute (braccio teso), adopted by fascists and Nazis, linking to Mussolini's Italy and Hitler's Germany, symbolizing imperial revival and authoritarianism. Together, these highlight Nazi appropriation of symbols from diverse cultures (Indian Svastika, Roman salute) and the human cost depicted in Maus, underscoring the need to combat cultural misuse in radicalization efforts.
