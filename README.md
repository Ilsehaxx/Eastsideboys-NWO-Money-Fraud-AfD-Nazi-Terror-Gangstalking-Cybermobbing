# GRU Influence Operation: Radicalizing German Youth via Neo-Nazis and Simson Mopeds

## Overview
This repository documents allegations of a Russian GRU (Main Intelligence Directorate) influence operation aimed at radicalizing youth in Germany. The operation reportedly leverages neo-Nazi symbols and the Simson moped brand to promote far-right extremism, particularly through the Alternative for Germany (AfD) party.

## Background on Simson Mopeds
- Founded in 1856 by Jewish brothers Löb and Moses Simson.
- The company was forced to sell to Nazis in the 1930s, and the family fled Germany.
- Simson mopeds became iconic in East Germany during the GDR era.
- In recent years, the AfD has appropriated the brand for political symbolism, riding Simson mopeds at events and using them in propaganda.
- Descendants of the Simson family have condemned this as "insulting" and "appropriation" of a Jewish heritage.

## AfD and Neo-Nazi Connections
- The AfD was classified as a "confirmed right-wing extremist" organization by Germany's domestic intelligence agency (BfV) in 2025.
- Leaders like Björn Höcke (Thuringia branch) have been noted for disseminating far-right ideology, including Nazi language.
- Höcke is frequently seen riding Simson mopeds at AfD promotional activities.

## Russian GRU Involvement

- GRU agents or proxies are co-opting angry young men in Europe and North America to promote anti-Western, pro-Russia messages.
- In Germany, this involves infiltrating far-right groups to radicalize youth, using symbols like Simson mopeds to evoke nostalgia and extremism.
- Russian neo-Nazis are participating in "denazifying" Ukraine, ironically, while supporting neo-Nazi groups elsewhere.
